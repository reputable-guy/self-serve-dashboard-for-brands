# The Demo That Sells Itself

*What makes a brand say "I NEED this" in 10 minutes?*

---

## The Core Insight

Brands already have star ratings. They have reviews. They have influencer testimonials.

**What they DON'T have:**
- Proof that their product actually works
- Before/after data from real customers
- Third-party verified results
- Stories they can use without looking like they wrote them

**The "aha moment":** When a brand executive sees a real customer's story â€” their struggle, their failed alternatives, their baseline data, and then their transformation with the product â€” backed by wearable data and third-party verification.

It's not "4.8 stars." It's *"Sarah hadn't slept through the night in 3 years. After 28 days with your product, her deep sleep increased 47%."*

That's what Trustpilot and Yotpo can't do.

## What to NAIL

### 1. Participant Stories (The Emotional Core)
- Card design must be beautiful and human
- Each card should feel like reading a mini-biography
- The wearable data should enhance the story, not overshadow it
- Progressive disclosure: story first, data second

### 2. Before/After Moment (The Proof)
- Visual. Not a table â€” a transformation.
- Side-by-side or animated transition
- Objective (wearable) + Subjective (testimonial) together
- Link to verification for credibility

### 3. The Widget (The Action)
- Must look like it belongs on a product page
- Auto-generated from study results
- One-click copy embed code
- Clicking through leads to full verification page

### 4. Speed to Value Message
- "First insights in 48 hours"
- "Full results in 4-6 weeks"
- Show the timeline visually

---

## Demo Data Quality

The simulation needs to feel REAL. Current issues:
- Generated names can feel fake
- Quotes need to feel like real human speech
- Scores and improvements need to be realistic, not too perfect
- Mix in some "meh" results (not everyone improves dramatically)

**Key:** Include at least one participant who saw minimal improvement. This paradoxically INCREASES credibility. A study where everyone improves 30%+ feels fake. A study where most improve and a few don't? That's science.
