## Tier System Context

Understanding tiers is critical for building appropriate demo content:

### Tier 1: Wearables Primary
**Categories:** Sleep, Recovery, Fitness

These demos MUST show impressive wearable metrics because that's the product's core value proposition.

**Demo expectation:** "Look at Sarah's Oura Ring data - her deep sleep went from 28 to 54 minutes."

**Metrics to show:**
- Sleep: Total sleep, deep sleep, REM, latency, efficiency, HRV
- Recovery: HRV, resting HR, sleep quality
- Fitness: Active minutes, VO2 max proxy, recovery scores

### Tier 2: Wearables Co-Primary
**Categories:** Stress

Wearables validate the subjective improvement. Both matter equally.

**Demo expectation:** "Marcus's HRV improved 47%, and he reports feeling calmer in meetings."

**Metrics to show:**
- Stress: HRV (higher = better), Resting HR (lower = better), stress assessment scores

### Tier 3: Assessment Primary, Wearables Supporting
**Categories:** Energy, Focus, Mood, Anxiety, Pain, Resilience

Assessments are the hero. Wearables (if connected) provide supporting validation.

**Demo expectation:** "Jennifer's anxiety score dropped from 72 to 31. Her sleep also improved according to her Fitbit."

**Metrics to show:**
- Primary: Category assessment composite score (0-100 scale)
- Supporting: HRV or sleep quality if wearable connected (optional)

### Tier 4: Assessment Only
**Categories:** Skin, Gut, Immunity, Hair, Weight, Libido, Satiety

No wearable metrics apply. Assessments and photo documentation are proof.

**Demo expectation:** "David's gut health score improved 62%. Here's his daily symptom log showing bloating decreased."

**Metrics to show:**
- Category assessment composite score
- Specific symptom tracking (bloating frequency, skin clarity rating, etc.)
- Photo documentation for visual categories (skin, hair, weight)

## Content Quality Guidelines

### Quote Writing Principles

1. **Specificity over generality**
   - Bad: "I've tried everything and nothing works"
   - Good: "I've tried three different probiotics, an elimination diet, and even got tested for SIBO"

2. **Emotional truth without melodrama**
   - Bad: "My life is ruined by bloating"
   - Good: "I avoid lunch meetings because I know I'll be uncomfortable by 2pm"

3. **Archetype consistency**
   - Desperate: Long-suffering, multiple failed attempts, high stakes
   - Skeptic: Data-driven, prove-it-to-me, references past disappointments
   - Power User: Already tracking, optimization mindset, wants marginal gains
   - Struggler: Relatable everyday person, moderate pain, wants simple solution
   - Optimist: Minor issue, preventive mindset, positive framing

4. **Category authenticity**
   - Use terminology actual customers use
   - Reference real products/approaches they've tried
   - Include category-specific symptoms and goals

5. **Compliance-safe language**
   - Avoid medical claims
   - Use "support" not "treat"
   - Focus on wellness, not diagnosis

### Journey Quote Principles

1. **Day 14 quotes** should show:
   - Early signs of change (or skepticism)
   - "Noticing something" language
   - Cautious optimism or continued doubt

2. **Day 28 quotes** should show:
   - Clear articulation of change
   - Specific examples ("I realized when...")
   - Future-looking statements

3. **Outcome distribution** (maintain across all categories):
   - 60% Improved: Clear positive change
   - 25% Mixed: Some improvement, some unchanged
   - 15% No Change: Honest "didn't work for me"
